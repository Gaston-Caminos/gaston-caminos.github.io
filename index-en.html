Computer Model and Analysis of a Concert Hall

This article details the modeling and acoustic simulation carried out in the EASE software of the Arthur Sykes Rymer Auditorium at the University of York in the United Kingdom. The STI, AlCons, RT, Echo Speech, Echo Music, Lateral Fraction, and D/R parameters are calculated and compared with the on-site measurements taken in the theater. In the simulation, a spatial mapping of the audience area is also carried out for the intelligibility, D/R, and direct sound pressure level parameters. With fine-tuning of the simulation model, the results obtained are consistent with the on-site measurements and are considerably similar, with some differences existing at some frequencies.

Algorithm for phase reconstruction based on the Griffin & Lim algorithm

This report details the processes for designing a phase reconstruction algorithm based on the Griffin-Lim method. It seeks to estimate a time signal from a modified magnitude of the short-time Fourier transform (STFT) given. The algorithm is designed so that in each iteration, the mean squared error between the estimated STFT and the provided modified STFT (MSTFT) decreases. The developed algorithm has been tested with signals of different origins such as musical instruments, spoken voice, and nature sounds; the process has proven to be effective with a minimal computational cost

Software Development for RIRs Processing

This study presents a software implementation for processing room impulse response designed in Python. The software is designed to be user-friendly, especially for users with little or no programming experience. The developed program is capable of obtaining room parameters such as RT20, RT30, EDT, Tt, EDTt, C50, C80, and IACC from a logarithmic sine sweep room response measurement. The results were compared with the popular Aurora Acoustical Parameters software, and it was concluded that the developed software is reliable, revealing a difference that does not exceed 5% in the calculation of temporal parameters within the valid frequency bands.

Design of a one-dimensional numerical diffuser

In this report, the design of a one-dimensional QRD11 diffuser is carried out to be used in a recording studio located in a neighborhood of Buenos Aires. It is specifically designed to operate in a frequency range of 165 Hz to 745 Hz. For this, the Schroeder method is used, using a period sequence of 11 with an ACQF of 0.18, a cell width of 23 cm, a number of levels equal to 5, and a maximum depth of 50 cm. The AFMG Reflex software is used to verify the theoretical results, and it is modeled in 3D using SketchUp. As a result, a diffuser with a total surface area of 3.8 m2 is obtained.

Redesign of the acoustics of a concert hall

This study proposes an acoustic redesign for the auditorium of the Palacio de Bellas Artes, located in Mexico City. The objective of this redesign is to improve the acoustic performance of the auditorium. A comprehensive study was carried out to understand the acoustic behavior, as well as the interaction of the building with its environment. The spatial location of the cultural center was analyzed to identify possible sources of noise, and the room to be redesigned was studied to improve the line of sight while respecting local regulations.

Valve preamplifier for guitar with harmonic generator

The present project arises from the proposal to design a valve preamplifier for an electric guitar. Subsequently, the need arose for this preamplifier to have the ability to generate harmonics, in other words, to be a valve distortion. Then, based on the idea that the preamplifier should have two different types of distortion, a stage with silicon diodes was added. The system has the ability to generate a passive blend of both stages at the output.
